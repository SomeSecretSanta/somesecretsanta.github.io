These four friends all meet at 5:30 in the morning at LAX (Los Angeles International Airport) on the 24th of December 2020 to have a little chat at a café before each of them travels to their destination. When each of them will reach their destination airport, they will use a rental car to drive to their winter resort.

Here is an overview of all flights on this day from that airport (adapted from the original Bureau of Transportation web site): 2020-12-24_LAX.ods, you can open it with Excel, LibreOffice and similar programs.

Each of the friends travel with their own sports equipment (which is different for everyone), and to a different location in the continental United States.

Your task is to find out their names (in real life), the destination airport of everyone, and (when they have reached their winter resort by rental car) their activity on site.

Tourists can do Nordic skiing, snowboarding, ice skating and also alpine skiing at each destination.

Click on the little squares in the table to mark them with an "X", "O" or make them empty again. This should help you when going through the clues below.

When you're confident that everything is correct, choose the names and destinations again in the table "Think you're got the solution". If it's really correct, then there might be a little reward for you.

All four friends were lucky, since none of their flights had a delay. Three of their planes even were a bit early, only the second flight started right on time.

One of the two men managed to only have one single piece of baggage - a standard large traveler bag.

The person traveling to Willamette Pass handed a little present to one of their friends: Tasty treats for their corgi "Omar".

The person going snowboarding left the café shortly after the first person left to catch their plane to Eugene airport.

The tallest of the four friends has the slimmest skis.

The distance to be traveled by the half-elf who has a brother in their campaign is the longest.

One friend promised the others to shout the name of their adventuring group “VOX MACHINA” at the top of their lungs when they arrive at their winter resort in Colorado to find out if the echo there is really so great that people named a lake and a mountain “Echo”.

Two of the friends flew with Delta Airlines.

One airline that one of the four friends uses is named after a US state.

One of the four friends was hoping to see the “Space Needle” shortly before their arrival at their destination airport.

After being alone for around four hours the last remaining person on the airport left the café to get to their plane which was scheduled at 11:55. They gave the waiter a 100 US$ bill saying “keep the change”. The waiter, surprised, since the sum only was 21 USD said: “Are you sure?” So the person said smiling and in character “I have an intelligence of 6, I know what I'm doing.”

First, try to find out the (real life) names of the four friends. Enter these names in any order into the four little text input fields at the top of the table. You'll need them when submitting your solution in the form below.

Second, deduce all destination airports and enter information about them into the text areas at the left of the table. Download the polished sheet mentioned in the first part "The Setup" above, with all important data on it, or (if you're feeling adventurous and want to spare no effort) use the original web site of the Bureau of Transportation Statistics, but I don't recommend it because of their really bad user interface. Do it only if you want to suffer like your secret santa did...

Oh, and it can't hurt if you know how to use Google Maps and you might need to use a simple web search if you don't know about the corgi. (But every real "critter" knows about Omar, right?)
